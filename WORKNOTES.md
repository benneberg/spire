# The sharp ICP definition:

The smallest, most specific group of customers who:

* feel the problem urgently
* already try to solve it
* can buy quickly
* and get disproportionate value from your product

⸻

What “sharp” actually means in practice:

A sharp ICP is defined across 4 layers:

1. Firmographics (baseline)

* Industry
* Size
* Geography

2. Technographics / stack

* What tools they already use
* What your product integrates with or replaces

3. Trigger events (critical)

* What just happened that makes them need you now

Examples:

* “Just raised Series A”
* “Migrating to microservices”
* “Scaling IoT device fleet past 10k units”

4. Pain intensity + behavior

* Are they actively solving this?
* Are they already spending money on it?
* Do they have internal ownership?

⸻

Example of a sharp ICP (compare carefully):

Blunt ICP:

IoT companies

Sharp ICP:

Mid-sized industrial IoT companies (50–200 employees) in Northern Europe that:

* manage 5k–50k deployed devices
* currently use AWS IoT Core but struggle with fleet observability
* recently expanded into multi-region deployments
* have a dedicated platform engineering team

Now you can:

* write messaging that hits
* find them fast
* close faster

⸻

How these two ideas connect

If you’re building infrastructure, a sharp ICP is how you become acquisition-worthy.

Because:

* Deep focus → strong product-market fit
* Strong PMF → high adoption in a niche
* High adoption + integration → strategic value
* Strategic value → acquisition interest

⸻

Practical takeaway (especially relevant for you)

If you’re working in IoT / systems:

* Don’t aim for “generic platform”
* Pick a painful, narrow wedge
* Build something that becomes:
    * deeply embedded
    * hard to replace
    * obviously valuable to a larger ecosystem player

That’s how infrastructure turns from “tool” → “asset someone will pay millions for”.
